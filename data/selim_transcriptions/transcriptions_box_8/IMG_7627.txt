Brief Summary: A letter from an unnamed official, likely from the Department of Antiquities Stores, to the Director General of the Egyptian Antiquities Service, submitting an invoice for L.E. 350.430 m/ms from M. Sayed Abdel Rahman Ibrahim Abdel Gawad for repair and restoration work on antiquities found at Saqqara and Luxor, and requesting payment. Subsequent annotations indicate the request was not submitted and was rejected.

1929/1/9
To His Excellency
The Director General of the Egyptian Antiquities Service
In Cairo
After greetings
And then
We request from His Excellency
The Director General of the Egyptian Antiquities Service

Mr. Director General,

I have the honor to submit herewith the invoice from Mr. Sayed Abdel Rahman Ibrahim Abdel Gawad, for the sum of L.E. 350.430 m/ms, for the cost of repair and restoration work on antiquities found at Saqqara and Luxor.

I would be grateful if you would kindly give the necessary orders for the payment of the aforementioned sum to Mr. Ibrahim Abdel Gawad from the works credit.

Please accept, Mr. Director General, the expression of my highest consideration.
