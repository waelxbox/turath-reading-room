Brief Summary: This document from the Director General of Antiquities informs the Undersecretary of State, Director General of Works, of immediate budget reductions for various archaeological projects, including those at Abu el Houl, Amenophis III's site, Abou Dos, and the Serapeum at Saqqara, due to the imminent exhaustion of unforeseen expense credits.
English Translation: Egyptian Antiquities Service
(Form No. 1528 "Double")
Subject:
Open credits for temple maintenance and repair works

27/4/25
Number of attachments:

Mr. Undersecretary of State
Director General of Works

It should be noted that due to the imminent exhaustion of the credit opened for unforeseen expenses, the Director General has decided the following:
- Firstly: Reduce the credit opened for the works of Abu el Houl by 400 pounds and bring it to 140/407 pounds only instead of 140/807 pounds.
- Secondly: Reduce the credit opened for the works of Amenophis III by 200 pounds and bring it to 200 pounds instead of 400 pounds. And if it is possible to postpone all the work until next year, there is no harm in canceling the entire credit.
- Thirdly: Reduce the credit opened for the works of Abou Dos by 100 pounds and bring it to 500 pounds.
- Fourthly: Cancel the amount of 070/307 pounds that was allocated to the works of the Serapeum at Saqqara.
- Fifthly: If the Service wishes to accelerate the execution of the open works, we ask you to inform us of this movement within fifteen days from April 1931, i.e., after the completion of the expenses of the first installment from April 1931.

And I, on this basis, kindly ask you to follow this.
And please accept, etc.

Cairo on 1/1/1931
Director General of Antiquities
H. Gauthier
7/1
