Brief Summary: Cecil M Firth provides architectural recommendations for lighting the Chapel of Ptahhotep, suggesting the use of narrow horizontal skylights or a central skylight to achieve appropriate illumination for the reliefs while ensuring ventilation and protection from elements.
English Translation: GENERAL
OF THE
ANTIQUITIES
(MODEL N° 37. — [Handwritten Arabic: Form number 37])

Cairo, February 25, 1922.

[Handwritten: 25-4/1]
ANTIQUITIES SERVICE.
OF EGYPT.
(MODEL N° 39 — [Handwritten Arabic: Form number 39])

This would ensure a lighting as in antiquity of Chapel
of Ptahhotep
Where however an external wall is not available
a good lighting could be obtained by means of long
narrow horizontal skylights of ribbed or obscured
glass (protected outside with wire netting) placed in the
roof above the reliefs it was desired to light. These
skylights could have one section hinged to open for
ventilation and the frames would be raised 5 centimetres
above surface of roof to prevent rain or sand accumulating
against them.
A central skylight could be used when preferable.

[Pencil note: glass]
[Pencil note: Curved joint]
[Pencil note: Roof]
[Pencil note: Wall relief]

Yours faithfully
Cecil M Firth
