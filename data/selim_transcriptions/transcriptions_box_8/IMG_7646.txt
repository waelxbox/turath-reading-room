Thematic Tags: Tomb Conservation, Archaeological Funding, Site Management, Rain Damage
Brief Summary: H. Davies writes to Pierre Lacau expressing concern over the lack of repair work on Tomb 23 at Kurneh, Luxor, despite a grant from Mr. Robert Mond, and urges prompt action by Monsieur Baraize before the rainy season to prevent further damage.
English Translation: KURNEH LUXOR
DEC.16/24

Mons. Pierre Lacau, of the Service des Antiquities, Cairo
Director-General

Sir, I have this week visited Tomb 23, and notice that nothing has been done to repair it except to collect a few bricks for the proposed roofing there. I understand that Mons. Baraize is coming here shortly to do work in the necropolis, and I hope that this work on Tomb 23 is to be one of his first tasks. I would remind you that the rainy season is due, and indeed threatened to begin to-day, and that the grant from the fund of Mr. Robert Mond was given expressly on the condition that the work was done at once, since the tomb has suffered so much of late years that another rain-storm would leave too little to be worth the expense of sheltering it.

I have the honour to be,
Yours very respectfully.

[Handwritten signature: H. Davies]
